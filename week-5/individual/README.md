# 📊 Operations Dashboard Design Project

## Overview
This is my individual Week 5 project.  
I created a simple Operations Dashboard in Power BI to analyze:

- **Sales by store location**
- **Inventory by category**

## Tools Used
- **SQL**
- **Power BI**
- **GitHub**

## Results and Business Insights

The dashboard shows that **Tallinn generates the largest share of total sales (37.54%)**, which makes it the strongest sales location. This suggests that Tallinn is the company’s key revenue driver and should remain an important focus for sales and marketing efforts.

The **Online channel** also contributes a large share of sales, showing that digital sales are an important part of the business. This may indicate good potential for further online growth and investment in e-commerce.

The inventory chart shows that **men’s clothing has the highest stock level**, while **accessories have the lowest**. From a business perspective, this helps identify how stock is distributed across categories and whether inventory levels match likely demand.

Overall, the dashboard supports decision-making by showing where the business earns most of its revenue and how inventory is currently allocated. This can help improve stock planning, sales focus, and resource allocation.
