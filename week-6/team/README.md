# Week 6 Team Combined View

## Overview

Our team analysis combined insights from physical stores and the e-commerce channel to better understand the company’s overall business performance. The results show that sales patterns differ across locations and channels, but several important trends can be seen across the business.

## Key Conclusions

- Sales are strongly affected by **seasonality**
- Summer and end-of-year periods generate the strongest results
- Some locations show clear **winter declines**, which creates off-season risk
- **E-commerce is one of the fastest-growing channels** and contributes a significant share of total revenue
- Physical stores remain important, but their performance varies by location, customer behavior, and retention
- A relatively small number of **top-performing products** generate a large share of sales

## Business Implications

- Seasonal planning is essential for campaigns, stock management, and sales forecasting
- The online channel should remain a strategic priority because of its strong growth potential
- Customer retention needs more attention in weaker-performing locations
- Product and inventory decisions should focus on best-selling items and seasonal demand patterns

## Final Insight

By combining insights from Tallinn, Tartu, Pärnu, and e-commerce, we gained a broader and more balanced view of the business. This combined analysis supports better decision-making in marketing, inventory planning, and long-term growth strategy.
